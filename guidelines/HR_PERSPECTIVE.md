# HR Perspective - What Recruiters Really Look For

## The HR Screening Process

### Time Allocation
- **6-10 seconds**: Initial resume scan
- **30-60 seconds**: Closer review if passes initial scan
- **2-3 minutes**: Detailed review of promising candidates

### Screening Volume
- **Average applications per role**: 100-250
- **Initial screening goal**: Narrow to 10-20 candidates
- **Phone screen goal**: Narrow to 5-10 candidates
- **Onsite interviews**: 3-5 candidates

## What HR Looks for in 6 Seconds

### The "Quick Scan" Checklist

✅ **First Glance**:
1. Clean, professional format
2. Name and contact info clear
3. Current/recent relevant title
4. Location match (if required)
5. No obvious red flags (gaps, job hopping)

**Decision Point 1**: Continue reading or move to next resume

✅ **Second Glance** (10-15 seconds):
1. Years of experience match requirement
2. Required skills present
3. Company names or brands recognized
4. Education meets minimum
5. Consistent progression

**Decision Point 2**: Add to "maybe" pile or reject

✅ **Closer Look** (30-60 seconds):
1. Quantified achievements
2. Relevant industry experience
3. Culture fit indicators
4. No concerning patterns
5. Overall impression positive

**Decision Point 3**: Move to phone screen or keep in maybe pile

## Red Flags That Cause Rejection

### Automatic Rejections

🚩 **Unprofessional Contact Information**:
- partygirl99@yahoo.com
- xxx_cooldude_xxx@gmail.com
- Instagram or TikTok handle as contact
**Why**: Indicates lack of professionalism

🚩 **Typos in Contact Info or Name**:
- Wrong phone format
- Misspelled own name
- Broken LinkedIn URL
**Why**: Attention to detail concern

🚩 **Photo Included (US Roles)**:
- Headshot on resume
- Full body photo
**Why**: Legal liability (age, appearance discrimination)

🚩 **Protected Class Information**:
- Age, birth date
- Marital status, children
- Religion, political affiliation
**Why**: Legal compliance - we can't consider this

🚩 **Wrong Company Name in Objective**:
> "Seeking position at [Competitor Name]"
**Why**: Copy-paste error shows lack of care

### Major Red Flags (Likely Rejection)

🚩 **Job Hopping** (without explanation):
- 5 jobs in 5 years
- Multiple <1 year stints
- Pattern of 6-month roles
**Concern**: Will they leave quickly?

🚩 **Large Unexplained Gaps**:
- 1+ year with no explanation
- Multiple long gaps
**Concern**: What happened? Are they still sharp?

🚩 **Backward Progression**:
- VP → Manager → Individual Contributor (unexplained)
- Senior → Junior title
**Concern**: What went wrong?

🚩 **Overqualified**:
- PhD applying for entry-level
- 20 years experience for junior role
- C-level applying for manager role
**Concern**: Will they be satisfied? Flight risk?

🚩 **Under-Qualified**:
- No degree when required
- 2 years experience when 5+ required
- Missing critical certifications
**Concern**: Can't do the job

🚩 **Formatting Issues**:
- Illegible font or size
- Poor formatting (messy, inconsistent)
- 5+ pages for non-executive role
**Concern**: Poor communication skills

### Moderate Red Flags (Need Explanation)

⚠️ **Frequent Company Changes**:
- Many different industries
- No progression pattern
- All contract roles (if applying for permanent)
**Question**: Why the instability?

⚠️ **Vague Job Descriptions**:
- Generic bullet points
- No specifics or metrics
- "Responsible for various tasks"
**Question**: What did they actually do?

⚠️ **All Buzzwords, No Substance**:
- "Synergized cross-functional stakeholders"
- "Leveraged cutting-edge solutions"
- "Thought leader" without evidence
**Question**: Are they all talk?

⚠️ **Outdated Skills**:
- Only old technologies (Flash, FoxPro)
- No recent learning
- Tech stack 10 years behind
**Question**: Are they current?

## What Makes HR Say "YES"

### Green Flags for Phone Screen

✅ **Perfect Match on Key Criteria**:
- Exact title or very close
- All required skills listed
- Required years of experience
- Required education/certifications
**Result**: Immediate phone screen

✅ **Brand Name Recognition**:
- Top-tier companies (FAANG, Fortune 500)
- Well-known startups
- Respected organizations in field
**Why**: Implies they passed rigorous hiring bar

✅ **Clear Progression**:
- Junior → Mid → Senior over time
- Increasing responsibility
- Promotions within companies
**Why**: Shows growth and value

✅ **Quantified Achievements**:
- Specific numbers and metrics
- Business impact clear
- Results-oriented language
**Why**: Shows they deliver results

✅ **Clean, Professional Presentation**:
- Easy to scan
- Well-organized
- No typos or errors
**Why**: Indicates attention to detail

✅ **Relevant Keywords**:
- Job description language used
- Industry terminology correct
- ATS-friendly format
**Why**: Shows they understand role

## HR Decision Framework

### Minimum Qualifications Check

**Must-Haves** (Non-negotiable):
- [ ] Years of experience (within range)
- [ ] Required education
- [ ] Critical certifications
- [ ] Must-have skills (core 3-5)
- [ ] Work authorization
- [ ] Location requirement

**If missing any**: Usually auto-reject

### Competitive Assessment

**Comparing candidates**:

**Tier 1** (Definitely Interview):
- Exceeds most requirements
- Brand name companies
- Strong quantified achievements
- Perfect cultural fit signals

**Tier 2** (Likely Interview):
- Meets all requirements
- Solid experience
- Good achievements
- No red flags

**Tier 3** (Maybe Pile):
- Meets minimum requirements
- Some gaps or questions
- Adequate but not standout
- Need more info

**Tier 4** (Reject):
- Doesn't meet minimums
- Significant red flags
- Poor presentation
- Not worth recruiter time

### Tiebreakers

When choosing between similar candidates:

1. **Internal referral** (always prioritized)
2. **More quantified results**
3. **Better culture fit signals**
4. **More recent relevant experience**
5. **Stronger brands on resume**
6. **Better written communication** (on resume)
7. **Diverse experience** (if valued)
8. **Growth trajectory** (promotions)

## HR Psychology

### What HR Cares About

**Primary Concerns**:
1. **Can they do the job?** (Skills match)
2. **Will they fit?** (Culture match)
3. **Will they stay?** (Retention risk)
4. **Are they hireable?** (No red flags)
5. **Will hiring manager approve?** (Safe bet)

**Not Primary Concerns**:
- Your career goals
- Your personal growth
- What you want to learn
- Why you need this job

**Harsh Truth**: HR's job is to protect company and find best candidates, not to help every applicant.

### How HR Thinks About Risk

**Low Risk** (Easy to advocate for):
- Similar role at similar company
- Clear progression
- No gaps or flags
- Strong references likely

**Medium Risk** (Need to justify):
- Career pivot
- Skill gaps
- Short tenures
- Overqualified

**High Risk** (Hard to advocate for):
- Multiple flags
- Significant gaps
- Big career change
- Unclear value

## HR Communication Signals

### What HR Says vs What It Means

| HR Says | What It Might Mean |
|---------|-------------------|
| "We'll keep your resume on file" | Not a match for this role |
| "You're overqualified" | Too expensive or flight risk |
| "We're looking for someone with more specific experience" | You don't have the exact skills |
| "We're moving forward with candidates who more closely match" | You're not in top tier |
| "We received many qualified applicants" | You're in maybe pile at best |
| "Let's schedule a phone screen" | You passed initial screening! |

### Response Times

**Quick Response** (<1 week):
- Often automated (ATS filters)
- Very strong match (top tier)
- Urgent hiring need

**Normal Response** (1-3 weeks):
- Standard screening process
- You're in the queue

**Slow Response** (3+ weeks):
- You're in maybe pile
- Position on hold
- High volume of candidates

**No Response**:
- Not a match
- ATS filtered out
- Position filled or cancelled

## How to Pass HR Screening

### Make HR's Job Easy

✅ **Use exact terminology from job posting**
- If they say "Java", don't just say "programming"
- If they want "5+ years", state your years clearly
- Match their language exactly

✅ **Make qualifications obvious**
- Don't make HR hunt for info
- Put critical skills prominently
- Use standard section headers

✅ **Answer the question**: "Can they do this job?"
- Show you have done similar work
- Quantify your relevant experience
- Demonstrate skills match

✅ **Eliminate concerns**
- Address gaps briefly (cover letter or resume)
- Explain job changes if pattern exists
- Show progression and growth

✅ **Professional presentation**
- Polished, error-free document
- Standard format (ATS-friendly)
- Appropriate length

### What HR Wants to See

1. **Clear headline/title** matching role
2. **Years of experience** stated clearly
3. **Required skills** in skills section
4. **Quantified achievements** in experience
5. **Recognizable companies** or context
6. **Steady progression** in roles
7. **Recent relevant work** highlighted
8. **Education credentials** clear
9. **No typos or errors**
10. **Professional contact info**

## For Different Career Situations

### Recent Graduates

**HR Concerns**:
- No experience
- Can they transition to professional environment?
- Will they stick around?

**How to Address**:
- Emphasize internships as real experience
- Highlight relevant coursework and projects
- Show leadership in campus activities
- Demonstrate eagerness to learn

### Career Changers

**HR Concerns**:
- Lack of direct experience
- Will training burden be too high?
- Are they serious or just exploring?

**How to Address**:
- Lead with transferable skills
- Show commitment (courses, projects, certifications)
- Explain the "why" in cover letter
- Frame change as strength

### Returning to Workforce

**HR Concerns**:
- Skills outdated?
- Can they handle pace?
- Family obligations?

**How to Address**:
- Show recent learning/updating
- Brief explanation of gap
- Emphasize readiness and commitment
- Recent freelance or volunteer work

### Over-Qualified Candidates

**HR Concerns**:
- Will they get bored?
- Can we afford them?
- Flight risk?

**How to Address**:
- Explain why this role interests you
- Show long-term thinking
- Address compensation expectations
- Emphasize mission/culture fit

## The HR Perspective Summary

**Remember**:
1. **HR is screening out**, not selecting finalists (that's hiring manager's job)
2. **First impression is critical** - you have seconds
3. **Red flags kill candidacy** faster than strengths boost it
4. **Make their job easy** - clear, professional, matches JD
5. **You're competing** with 100+ other resumes
6. **ATS comes first** - must pass robot before reaching human
7. **Keywords matter** - exact matches boost ranking
8. **Professional presentation** shows respect for process
9. **Quantified results** stand out in sea of duties
10. **One typo** can end your candidacy

**Bottom Line**: HR's perspective is "Why NOT this person?" rather than "Why this person?" Your resume must eliminate every possible reason to reject you before your strengths even get considered.
